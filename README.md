# Bright-Coffee-Shop-Sales-Analysis
For CEO

In this project, The Bright Coffee Analysis was conducted to evaluate the performance of the business over a six-month period, using transactional data extracted from all three Coffee shops (Atoria, Lower Manhattan and Hell's Kitchen). This analysis will provide a comprehensive view of sales activity, customer behavior, and revenue performance, enabling data driven insights to support strategic decision making for the CEO. The goal is to understand key business drivers, identify growth opportunities, and improve overall operational efficiency.

--- Objectives---

- To analyze revenue performance across all coffee shops over six months
- To identify sales trends and peak trading periods
- To determine top performing products and shops
- To evaluate customer purchasing patterns
- To provide actionable insights for business growth and optimization

--- Tools Used---

- Miro: for mind mapping and plannery for the project
- Databricks: for data extraction, processing, and SQL analysis
- Microsoft Excel: for data cleaning, pivot tables, calculations and graphs
- Canva: for presenting insights to stakeholders

--- Project Tasks---

- Extracted and consolidated 6 months of transactional data from all 3 coffee shops
- Performed SQL analysis to calculate revenue, sales trends, and performance metrics
- Created pivot tables and dashboards to visualize key insights
- Analyzed time-based sales patterns (morning, afternoon, evening) known as time classifications
- Identified tops selling products and highest performing branch
- Compiled findings into a professional presentation

--- Recommendations---

- Leverage peak hours (morning and late afternoon) with targeted promotions and combo deals
- Boost evening sales through discounts, marketing campaigns
- Focus on high-performing products (coffee, bakery, and tea) to maximize revenue
